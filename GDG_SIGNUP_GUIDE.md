# Google Developer Group (GDG) Signup Guide

*For hackathon bonus points*

## What is GDG?

Google Developer Groups (GDG) are community groups for developers interested in Google's developer technology. Benefits include:
- Access to Google experts
- Networking opportunities
- Workshops and events
- Early access to new technologies
- Community support

## How to Sign Up

### Step 1: Visit GDG Website

Go to: **https://developers.google.com/community/gdg**

### Step 2: Find Your Local Chapter

1. Click "Find a GDG near you"
2. Enter your location
3. Browse available chapters
4. Choose the one closest to you

### Step 3: Join on Meetup.com

Most GDGs use Meetup.com:

1. Click "Join this group" on the GDG page
2. Create a Meetup.com account (if you don't have one)
3. Complete your profile
4. Join the GDG chapter

### Step 4: Create Your Public Profile

1. Go to your Meetup.com profile
2. Make sure it's set to "Public"
3. Add a profile photo
4. Add your bio mentioning:
   - Your interest in Google technologies
   - That you're participating in #GeminiLiveAgentChallenge
   - Your tech stack (Gemini, Google Cloud, etc.)

### Step 5: Get Your Profile Link

Your public GDG profile URL will be:
```
https://www.meetup.com/[your-username]/
```

Or find it by:
1. Going to your Meetup profile
2. Clicking "Share profile"
3. Copying the public link

## Alternative: Google Developer Profile

You can also create a Google Developer Profile:

1. Visit: **https://developers.google.com/**
2. Click "Sign in" (top right)
3. Use your Google account
4. Complete your developer profile
5. Add your projects and interests
6. Make profile public

Your profile URL will be:
```
https://developers.google.com/profile/u/[your-id]
```

## Tips for Engagement

To maximize your GDG membership:

1. **Attend Events**: Join virtual or in-person meetups
2. **Participate**: Ask questions, share knowledge
3. **Network**: Connect with other developers
4. **Share Your Project**: Present CareerPilot AI at a meetup
5. **Stay Active**: Engage with the community regularly
