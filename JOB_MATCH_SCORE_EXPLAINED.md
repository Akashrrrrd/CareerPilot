# Job Match Score - How It Works

## Overview
The match score (shown as a percentage like "95% match") indicates how well a job posting aligns with what makes a good opportunity. It's calculated based on multiple factors to help you prioritize which jobs to apply to first.

## Scoring Breakdown (Total: 100 points)

### 1. Recency (Max 20 points)
Jobs posted recently are more likely to still be actively hiring:
- **Posted today (0 days)**: +20 points
- **Within 2 days**: +15 points
- **Within a week**: +10 points
- **Within 2 weeks**: +5 points
- **Older than 2 weeks**: 0 points

**Why it matters**: Fresh postings have less competition and faster response times.

### 2. Job Completeness (Max 15 points)
Complete job postings show the company is serious and organized:
- **Has salary information**: +5 points
- **Detailed description (200+ chars)**: +5 points
- **Lists qualifications/requirements**: +3 points
- **Lists benefits**: +2 points

**Why it matters**: Complete postings mean transparent employers and help you make informed decisions.

### 3. Company Reputation (Max 5 points)
More job details and extensions indicate an established company:
- **Multiple job details/extensions**: +5 points

**Why it matters**: Established companies often have better hiring processes and resources.

### 4. Base Score (60 points)
Every job starts with 60 points, ensuring even basic postings get a reasonable score.

### 5. Variety Factor (±5 points)
A small random adjustment to add variety and prevent all jobs from having identical scores.

**Why it matters**: Prevents score clustering and encourages exploring different opportunities.

## Score Ranges & What They Mean

### 🟢 85-100% - Excellent Match
- Recently posted (within days)
- Complete job information
- Salary disclosed
- Clear requirements and benefits
- **Action**: Apply immediately - these are hot opportunities!

### 🔵 70-84% - Good Match
- Posted within 1-2 weeks
- Most information available
- May be missing salary or some details
- **Action**: Review carefully and apply if interested

### 🟡 60-69% - Fair Match
- Older posting or incomplete information
- Basic details only
- May require more research
- **Action**: Consider if it aligns with your goals

### 🔴 Below 60% - Lower Priority
- Very old posting or minimal information
- May be less active or organized
- **Action**: Apply only if it's exactly what you want

## Important Notes

### What Match Score Does NOT Consider:
- Your specific skills or experience
- Your location preferences
- Your salary expectations
- Company culture fit
- Your career goals

### Why?
The match score focuses on **job posting quality** rather than personal fit because:
1. We don't have access to your detailed profile/resume
2. Personal fit is subjective and varies by individual
3. You're the best judge of whether a job fits your goals

## How to Use Match Scores

### Best Practice:
1. **Sort by match score** to see the freshest, most complete postings first
2. **Read the full description** - a 95% match doesn't mean it's perfect for YOU
3. **Apply to 70%+ matches** that align with your skills and goals
4. **Don't ignore lower scores** if the job title/company excites you

### Pro Tips:
- High match scores = less competition (newer postings)
- Jobs with salary info = more transparent employers
- Complete postings = better organized companies
- Apply within 48 hours of posting for best results
